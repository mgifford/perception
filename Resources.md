# Best “Same Stimulus, Different Perception” Videos to Use

These videos illustrate how people can observe the same stimulus yet experience different perceptions due to attention limits, expectation effects, sensory integration, or post-hoc rationalization.

---

## 1. Inattentional Blindness

### Invisible Gorilla (Simons & Chabris)
**What it demonstrates:**  
When focused on a counting task, many people fail to notice an unexpected but obvious event.

**Why it works:**  
It creates an immediate, personal experience of perceptual overconfidence.

**Video:**  
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vJG698U2Mvo

---

### Monkey Business Illusion (Daniel Simons)
**What it demonstrates:**  
A variation on the gorilla experiment that often surprises even those familiar with the original.

**Why it works:**  
Prevents the “I already know this trick” reaction and reinforces inattentional blindness.

**Video:**  
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IGQmdoK_ZfY

---

## 2. Change Blindness

### The Door Study (Simons & Levin)
**What it demonstrates:**  
People fail to notice when a person they are speaking to is swapped with someone else.

**Why it works:**  
Shows that large changes in real-world situations can go unnoticed.

**Video:**  
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FWSxSQsspiQ

---

### NOVA / PBS Change Blindness Demo
**What it demonstrates:**  
Obvious scene changes often go undetected.

**Why it works:**  
Professional production value and clear explanation of the cognitive mechanism.

**Video:**  
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VkrrVozZR2c

---

## 3. Sensory Construction

### The McGurk Effect
**What it demonstrates:**  
Visual input changes what people hear, even when the audio track is identical.

**Why it works:**  
Shows that perception is constructed, not passively recorded.

**Video (BBC version):**  
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=G-lN8vWm3m0

---

## 4. Expectation and Auditory Ambiguity

### Yanny vs Laurel
**What it demonstrates:**  
Identical audio produces different perceived words depending on listener and frequency emphasis.

**Why it works:**  
Clear example of two sincere perceptual realities from the same stimulus.

**Video:**  
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QH9jKxjm2us

---

### Green Needle vs Brainstorm
**What it demonstrates:**  
Expectation changes what people report hearing.

**Why it works:**  
Shows priming effects directly altering perception.

(Various versions available on YouTube.)

---

## 5. Choice Blindness

### Petter Johansson – TED Talk
**What it demonstrates:**  
People confidently justify choices they did not actually make.

**Why it works:**  
Transitions from perception to post-hoc rationalization and interpretive confidence.

**Video:**  
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tuEGoAabL9o

---

## 6. Ambiguous Figure-Ground Perception

### Rubin’s Vase (Faces vs Vase)
**What it demonstrates:**  
An image can flip between two stable interpretations.

**Why it works:**  
Illustrates how the brain selects one interpretation at a time.

**Example Video:**  
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7-hneeCt-60

---

# Suggested Educational Sequence

1. Invisible Gorilla  
2. Monkey Business  
3. Door Study  
4. McGurk Effect  
5. Yanny vs Laurel  
6. Choice Blindness  
7. Transition to real-world political/news footage  

This progression moves from simple attention failures to identity-shaped interpretation, providing a scaffolded foundation before introducing real-world disagreement.
